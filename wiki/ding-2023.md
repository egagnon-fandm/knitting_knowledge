# Ding, Sanchez, Bertoldi, Rycroft — Unravelling the Mechanics of Knitted Fabrics Through Hierarchical Geometric Representation (2023)

**Summary**: A yarn-based dynamical simulation framework using cubic B-splines and Lagrangian mechanics to study the nonlinear elastic response and anisotropy of weft-knitted fabrics, with experimental validation and demonstration of programmable multi-structure composites.

**Sources**: `raw/Unravelling the Mechanics of Knitted Fabrics Through.pdf`

**Last updated**: 2026-04-13

## Citation

Ding, X., Sanchez, V., Bertoldi, K., & Rycroft, C. H. (2023). Unravelling the mechanics of knitted fabrics through hierarchical geometric representation. arXiv:2307.12360v2 [cond-mat.soft]. October 3, 2023.

Affiliations: Harvard SEAS, University of Wisconsin–Madison (Mathematics), Stanford (Chemical Engineering), Lawrence Berkeley Laboratory.

## Key contributions

1. Yarn-based **dynamical** simulation (vs. energy minimization in [[dimitriyev-notes-2019]] / [[singal-naturecomm-2024]]).
2. Uses **cubic B-splines** (vs. Bézier curves in other frameworks); yarn is **extensible** (not inextensible).
3. Includes **friction damping** at yarn-yarn contacts — important for realistic quasi-static behavior.
4. Accounts for **manufacturing pre-tension** via a preliminary ramping simulation.
5. Builds **full fabric assemblies** (not just unit cells), enabling multi-structure composites.
6. Identifies **yarn rearrangement dynamics** (alignment) as the origin of anisotropy in regime 1.

## Geometric framework

Each yarn centerline is a cubic B-spline with N segments:

$$\mathbf{y}(s, t) = \sum_{k=-1}^{N+1} b_k(s) \mathbf{q}_k(t)$$

where $b_k(s) = B(s-k)$ are translates of the cubic B-spline basis $B(s)$. Total DOF per yarn: $3m = 3(N+3)$, stored as vector $\mathbf{q}$.

Loop initial shape parametrized by:
$$\mathbf{y}_p(w) = \begin{pmatrix} \lambda_x(w + \sin(\pi w)) \\ \lambda_y \cos(\pi w/2) \\ \lambda_z \cos(\pi w) \end{pmatrix}$$
with scaling factors $\lambda_x, \lambda_y, \lambda_z$ controlling aspect ratio.

## Lagrangian dynamics

Equation of motion for each control point $\mathbf{q}_k$:
$$\frac{d}{dt}(\nabla_{\dot{\mathbf{q}}_k} T) + \nabla_{\mathbf{q}_k} V + \nabla_{\dot{\mathbf{q}}_k} D = 0$$

**Kinetic energy**: $T(\dot{\mathbf{q}}) = \frac{\rho l}{2} \int_\Omega \mathbf{v}^\mathbf{T} \mathbf{v}\, ds$ — generates banded mass matrix $M$.

**Potential energy**: $V = V^s + V^b + V^g$

- Stretching: $V^s = \frac{E^s A l}{2} \int_\Omega \left(\frac{\|\mathbf{y}'\|}{l} - 1\right)^2 ds$ — linear elasticity, yarn is extensible
- Bending: $V^b = \frac{E^b I l}{2} \int_\Omega \kappa^2\, ds$ where $\kappa = \frac{\|\mathbf{y}' \times \mathbf{y}''\|}{\|\mathbf{y}'\|^3}$
- Gravity: $V^g = \rho l \int_\Omega \mathbf{y}^\mathbf{T} \mathbf{g}\, ds$

## Yarn-yarn contact and friction

Contact between yarn segments $i$ and $j$ via discrete sphere model:
$$V_{i,j}^\text{con} = l^2 \sum_{\alpha=1}^n \sum_{\beta=1}^n f\!\left(\frac{\|\mathbf{y}_i(s_\alpha) - \mathbf{y}_j(s'_\beta)\|}{d_\text{con}}\right)$$
where $f(\delta) = k(\delta-1)^2$ for $\delta < 1$, zero otherwise.

Friction damping approximated as:
$$D_{i,j}^\text{fri} = l^2 \sum_{\alpha,\beta} \left(k_{dt}\|\Delta\mathbf{v}_{\alpha\beta}\|^2 - (k_{dt}-k_{dn})(\hat{\mathbf{n}}_{\alpha\beta}^\mathbf{T} \Delta\mathbf{v}_{\alpha\beta})^2\right)$$
with tangential constant $k_{dt}$ and normal constant $k_{dn}$.

## Comparison with other simulation frameworks

| Feature | Ding 2023 | Singal/Dimitriyev |
|---|---|---|
| Yarn representation | Cubic B-spline | Bézier (cubic or degree-5) |
| Dynamics | Full Lagrangian ODE | Energy minimization |
| Yarn extensibility | Extensible (linear elasticity) | Inextensible |
| Contact model | Discrete spheres (soft) | Hertz repulsion |
| Friction | Yes | No |
| Scale | Full fabric | Unit cell |
| Pre-tension | Explicit ramping simulation | Not explicitly addressed |
| Implementation | C++ + OpenMP | Python + scipy |

## Fabric assembly and pre-tension

Four weft-knit patterns studied: jersey (= stockinette), garter 1×1, rib 1×1, seed 1×1.

Fabric built row by row, with spiral "cast-on" and "bind-off" yarns at top and bottom boundaries. Pre-tension applied by reducing yarn rest length $l(t)$ linearly from $l_0$ to $\eta l_0$ during a preliminary simulation, matching experimentally measured fabric dimensions.

## Results

### Stress-strain behavior (warp / wale / y direction)

| Fabric | Regime 1 stiffness | Regime 1 range | Regime 2 stiffness |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jersey | ~0.1 MPa | up to ~30% | monotonically increasing (10×) |
| Rib | ~2× jersey | up to ~40% | higher slope, fails at 60% |
| Seed | similar to rib | up to ~50% | ~1 MPa asymptote |
| Garter | ~0.01 MPa (softest) | up to ~60% | ~1 MPa, failure ~90% |

**Weft (course / x) direction**: roles flip — rib becomes initially softest (>100% strain in regime 1); garter becomes stiffest and least extensible.

### Yarn rearrangement (anisotropy mechanism)

Rose diagrams of yarn segment alignment angles quantify how segments progressively rotate toward the loading direction under stress. Fabrics that are more "unaligned" with the loading direction have more geometric degrees of freedom available → softer regime 1 response. This is a topological/geometric effect, not a material one.

- Garter: <10% segments initially aligned with warp → very soft under warp tension
- Rib: >20% segments initially aligned with warp → stiff under warp, soft under weft

## Design space demonstration

- 2D multi-structure patches combining garter and jersey (or jersey and rib) produce localized compliance gradients
- 3D composite tubes (jersey with varying material stiffness) actuate into bent configurations under axial tension
- Model can compute micromechanical hot spots via stretching energy field on individual yarn segments

## Implementation details

- C++ with OpenMP multithreading
- Adaptive 4th-order Runge-Kutta integration (FSAL method); adaptive timestep
- Mass matrix $M$ precomputed (banded, solved via LAPACK `dgbtrf`/`dgbtrs`)
- Contact detected via spatial binning (O(1) per sphere)
- Simulations use 13 wales × 12 courses; experiments use 41 wales × 40 courses; dimensionless comparison via aspect ratio and normalized stiffness

## Relationship to other sources

- Extends yarn-level B-spline approach of Kaldor et al. (computer graphics) to physical mechanics
- Parallels Singal 2024 ([[singal-naturecomm-2024]]) in confirming J-shape, ordering of fabric stiffness
- Adds dynamic + friction layer not present in [[yarn-simulation]] (Dimitriyev)
- Pre-tension treatment provides a principled link between manufacturing and mechanical reference state
